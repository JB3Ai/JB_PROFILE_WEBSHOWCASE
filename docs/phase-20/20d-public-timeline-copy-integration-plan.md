## Proposed Public Timeline Copy

The copy below is planning copy only. It is not approved for direct website integration until human review confirms wording, visual fit, and safety gates.

### Elite Technologies

Short label: `Signal: Elite Technologies`

One-line version: Elite Technologies was Jonathan Blackburn's historical smart-home, AV, automation, and control-systems chapter: the first proof that complexity could be made usable.

50-word version: Elite Technologies was Jonathan Blackburn's historical smart-home, AV, automation, and control-systems chapter. Built around connected environments, touchscreen control, boardroom-style spaces, and practical technical delivery, it became the Signal phase of the founder story: the first proof that complexity could be turned into usable systems for people.

100-word version: Elite Technologies was Jonathan Blackburn's early technical founder chapter: a historical smart-home, AV, automation, and control-systems business focused on making complex spaces easier to operate. The work connected audio-visual systems, touchscreen interfaces, iPad-style control, automation logic, and high-trust delivery into practical environments people could actually use. In the wider founder arc, Elite represents the Signal phase: the first proof that complexity could be organized, controlled, and made useful. Its relevance today is the systems foundation it created before the later diagnostics, laboratory, and intelligent systems chapters.

Safety notes:

- Keep Elite Technologies historical.
- Do not imply current operation.
- Do not name clients, homes, boardrooms, installations, access systems, or private environments.
- Do not include security, configuration, or sensitive technical details.

Required review before integration:

- Human historical wording review.
- Visual fit review if used in Timeline / Phase System.
- Confirmation that no current business-service claim is created by surrounding copy.

### Trust In Hope

Short label: `Structure: Trust In Hope`

One-line version: Trust In Hope was the historical recovery and service chapter, showing that systems are not only technical; they can also help people rebuild.

50-word version: Trust In Hope was Jonathan Blackburn's historical recovery and service chapter: a structured environment built around routine, accountability, care, service, life skills, and reintegration. It became the Structure phase of the founder arc, moving systems thinking from connected spaces into human rebuilding without exposing private recovery stories.

100-word version: Trust In Hope was Jonathan Blackburn's historical recovery and service chapter, created around structure, accountability, care, and the daily work of rebuilding after primary treatment. The public-safe story is not private recovery detail. It is the systems lesson: routine, weekly planning, meetings, service, life skills, reintegration support, and accountability created a framework people could return to each day. In the founder arc, Trust In Hope became the Structure phase: the point where systems thinking moved from technical environments into human rebuilding, and where care became stronger because it had rhythm and accountability.

Safety notes:

- Keep Trust In Hope historical.
- Avoid resident names, identifying stories, rates, contacts, operational rules, addresses, or vulnerable-person details.
- Avoid clinical, medical, treatment, or outcome claims.
- Keep recovery language dignified, general, and systems-led.

Required review before integration:

- Human sensitivity review.
- Recovery/public-safety wording review.
- Confirmation that copy does not invite curiosity into private resident or recovery stories.

### iMED

Short label: `Precision: iMED`

One-line version: iMED was the historical diagnostics and laboratory systems chapter, moving the founder arc into process discipline, precision, and operational clarity.

50-word version: iMED was Jonathan Blackburn's historical diagnostics and laboratory systems chapter, connecting rapid diagnostics, forensic toxicology, compliance, COVID-response capability, and DNA/forensic expansion. It became the Precision phase: the movement from human recovery structures into evidence-led systems where reliability, process discipline, and operational clarity mattered.

100-word version: iMED Medical Group was Jonathan Blackburn's historical diagnostics and laboratory systems chapter, shaped by testing and reliability gaps that became visible after the Trust In Hope era. Where Trust In Hope focused on structure and human rebuilding, iMED moved the founder arc into evidence-led systems: rapid diagnostics, forensic toxicology, laboratory operations, compliance discipline, COVID-response capability, and DNA/forensic expansion. In the wider story, iMED became the Precision phase. It strengthened the founder's respect for documentation, repeatable process, careful handling, grounded claims, and systems that reduce uncertainty without overstating what they can prove.

Safety notes:

- Keep iMED historical.
- Do not imply current operation, current accreditation, active licensing, current services, or available testing.
- Do not include patient, client, forensic case, lab access, regulatory, legal, financial, liquidation, or confidential data.
- Avoid scientific, clinical, regulatory, commercial, or outcome overclaims.

Required review before integration:

- Human historical/compliance wording review.
- Check surrounding page copy for active-service implication.
- Confirm no current iMED service, testing, accreditation, licensing, or regulatory claim is created.

## Avoid / Do Not Use Language

| Prohibited or risky phrase type | Why risky | Safer alternative |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "currently operates" | Creates current business/service claims | "was a historical chapter" |
| "current accredited lab" | Implies current accreditation or regulatory status | "historical diagnostics and laboratory systems chapter" |
| "available testing services" | Implies active test availability | "historical diagnostics capability" |
| "active recovery facility" | Implies current operation and sensitive service status | "historical recovery and service chapter" |
| "residents" | Can expose or invite vulnerable-person context | "people rebuilding" or "the service environment" |
| "patients" | Creates medical/privacy and current-service risk | "people" or "the historical diagnostics context" |
| "court-ready proof" | Suggests legal/evidence claims and forensic-case specificity | "evidence-led systems" |
| "award-winning" unless verified | Unsupported recognition claim | "recognition material" or "credibility context" |
| "first" or "only" unless historical and source-supported | Overclaiming or unverifiable category leadership | "early", "historical", or "one chapter in the founder arc" |
| Any claim suggesting GTR³ exists or has arrived | Breaks symbolic/aspirational boundary | "GTR³ remains symbolic and aspirational" |
| "currently offers forensic testing" | Active service and regulatory implication | "historical forensic toxicology capability" |
| "treatment outcomes" | Medical/recovery outcome claim | "routine, accountability, care, and reintegration" |
