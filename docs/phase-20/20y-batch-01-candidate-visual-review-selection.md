## Product-by-Product Visual Review

### A. OS³ Dash

The v02 OS³ Dash candidate resolves the visible `WOORFLOW` typo by removing readable labels from the primary dashboard surface. The visual now reads as a premium command-dashboard concept with abstract panels, signal paths, review nodes, and a central operating surface.

No readable Gmail, Drive, client, family, legal, financial, credential, or real workflow content was observed during visual review. The image has a strong OS³ command-dashboard feel and fits the premium founder OS direction from 20P/20Q.

Desktop crop is likely strong because the central panel and surrounding command surface stay balanced. The 390px mobile crop is conditional: the image should be centred on the main dashboard plate and not rely on tiny peripheral details.

Recommendation: selected for later optimisation review, subject to human crop QA and a final check that tiny marks are abstract rather than meaningful private data.

### B. JB³Ai Super Agent OS

The v02 JB³Ai Super Agent OS candidate avoids the previous incorrect `JB3Ai` title by removing prominent title text. This is the safest route because generated image text may not reliably render superscript brand characters. If title text appears in any future candidate, it must use `JB³Ai`, and platform naming must use `OS³`.

The visual communicates a human-led command layer with surrounding specialist workflow pods. It does not read as a cartoon chatbot directory, and it does not visibly expose internal prompts, private workflow labels, client data, or architecture secrets.

The remaining risks are interpretive rather than textual: tiny UI glyphs, lock/shield-like icons, and scale cues could be read as stronger security or automation claims than intended. The no-title approach is acceptable for optimisation review if the later card copy carries the exact public-safe product name.

Desktop crop is likely strong. The 390px mobile crop is conditional because outer workflow pods may lose detail, but the central command figure and routing architecture remain clear.

Recommendation: selected for later optimisation review, subject to human architecture-meaning review, claim/scale review, and crop QA.

### C. ClipboardAi

The retained ClipboardAi v01 candidate remains a clean private-workspace concept. It uses generic cards, simple stages, and an action queue without visible names, emails, files, document contents, attachments, or sensitive data.

The visible labels are generic and safe: `Capture`, `Structure`, `Review`, `Share`, and `Action`. No unsupported encryption guarantee, security claim, or confidential workspace content was observed.

Desktop crop is strong. The 390px mobile crop should work if focused on the central cards and action sequence. This is the lowest-risk candidate in the Batch 01 set because it is clear, restrained, and not heavily dependent on third-party references.

Recommendation: selected for later optimisation review, subject to final human check for text legibility and private-workspace resemblance.

### D. VoiceGrid Ai

The retained VoiceGrid Ai v01 candidate is visually strong and clearly communicates a communication layer through a central voice node, routing paths, support points, review points, and handoff flow.

No phone numbers, transcripts, recordings, call logs, customer names, or specific telecom data were observed. The main risk is the multilingual cue stack on the left, which includes visible greetings in multiple languages. These are suggestive rather than operational, but they could imply unsupported language coverage if used without careful copy.

Desktop crop is strong. The 390px mobile crop is conditional because the left-side language labels may become small while still carrying claim risk. The visual is promising but should not move to optimisation until human claims review decides whether to keep, crop away, blur, or regenerate the multilingual cue area.

Recommendation: held for human review. Do not advance to optimisation review yet.

### E. DukeBox of London

The v02 DukeBox of London candidate resolves the 20X3 title/text problem by avoiding readable title text. It has a strong creative music-generation showcase feel: cinematic console, waveform sculpture, premium studio atmosphere, and no obvious streaming/download interface.

No copyrighted album art, artist likeness, label logo, track list, catalog, or distribution UI was observed. The remaining concern is the London landmark-style skyline on the left. It gives the image a clear London cue, but it should receive human rights/landmark-style review before optimisation.

Desktop crop is visually strong. The 390px mobile crop is conditional because the landmark cue may crop awkwardly or become visually dominant depending on focal area.

Recommendation: held for rights/landmark-style review before optimisation. The candidate is visually strong, but not yet cleared for optimisation review.
