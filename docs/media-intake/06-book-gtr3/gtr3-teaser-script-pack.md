## Creative Direction

The GTR³ teaser should feel cinematic, restrained, resilient, and disciplined.
It should use dark motion, precise pacing, sparse text, low-frequency sound, and
a sense of controlled anticipation rather than triumph.

The message is not that the third GT-R has arrived. The message is that the
third rise is being built. GTR³ should remain a book, founder journey, and
loading-state symbol: fall, rise, rebuild, evolve.

Visual language:

- Dark cinematic frame.
- Subtle progress/loading motif.
- Controlled movement rather than speed flex.
- Founder-system language instead of car ownership language.
- Chrome, shadow, pulse, and quiet tension.
- Minimal text with enough pause to read.

Message pillars:

- Three rebuilds.
- Fall. Rise. Rebuild. Evolve.
- Structure before hope.
- AI for good.
- Father/founder resilience, referenced generally without private family detail.
- GTR³ as book, story, and loading symbol.
- Not arrival. Evolution.

### Visual Notes

Open on darkness, a faint chrome line, or a loading bar motif. Let the image
reveal slowly rather than cutting fast. Use one controlled push-in or subtle
motion pass. Keep the car imagery symbolic and atmospheric, never celebratory or
ownership-driven.

### Sound / Music Notes

Low cinematic pulse, restrained sub-bass, soft mechanical texture, and one
quiet rise into the ending card. Avoid aggressive engine revs or victory music.

### Safety Notes

- Do not imply the third GT-R exists or has been obtained.
- Do not frame the teaser as a victory announcement.
- Keep GTR³ symbolic, aspirational, and connected to rebuilding.
